Julius Robert Oppenheimer

father of the atomic bomb

Julius Robert Oppenheimer, often referred to as "J. Robert Oppenheimer," was a brilliant American theoretical physicist who played a pivotal role in the development of the atomic bomb during World War II

Here's a time line of Julius Robert Oppenheimer's life :

  • 1904 -

    Born on April 22 in New York City.

  • 1922 -

    Began studies at Harvard University, initially majoring in chemistry.

  • 1925 -

    Switched to physics and graduated from Harvard.

  • 1926-1927 -

  • 1929 -

    Returned to the United States and began his academic career.

  • 1930s

    Became a physics professor at the University of California, Berkeley. Conducted important research in quantum physics.

  • 1942

    Appointed as the director of the Los Alamos Laboratory, the center for atomic bomb development in the Manhattan Project.

  • 1945

    The first atomic bomb was tested at Trinity Site, New Mexico. Oppenheimer witnessed the test and uttered the famous quote from the Bhagavad Gita, "Now I am become Death, destroyer of worlds.

  • 1945

    Atomic bombs were dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, ending World War II.

  • Post-War

    Became a controversial figure due to his role in developing nuclear weapons.

  • 1954

    Lost his security clearance due to accusations of having ties to the Communist Party.

  • 1963

    Received the Enrico Fermi Award.

  • 1967

    Died on February 18 in Princeton, New Jersey.
